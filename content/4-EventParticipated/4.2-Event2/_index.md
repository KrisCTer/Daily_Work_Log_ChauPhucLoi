# Summary Report: “AWS Knowledge Battle”

### Event Objectives

- Provide a highly interactive and explosive AWS Trivia Playground.
- Test participants' AWS knowledge under strict time pressure.
- Create a fun networking opportunity for the AWS enthusiast community.

### Competition Format

- **Participants**: 6 teams competing head-to-head.
- **Matchups**: Teams were divided into 1-vs-1 direct elimination brackets.
- **Progression**: Winning teams continued deep into the bracket until the Finals (currently postponed).

### Key Highlights

#### Question Content and Difficulty
- The exam was divided into 3 difficulty levels, ranging from basic AWS knowledge to architectural design thinking.
- The hardest "Advanced" level took up only 1-2 questions per round but served as the key tie-breaker.

#### Decision Making Under Time Pressure
- Competing with a countdown clock forced quick reactions to architectural concepts (e.g., DynamoDB Partition keys, SQS Visibility Timeouts).

#### Identifying Knowledge Gaps
- Smoothly handled heavy Backend questions due to real-world experience (PeriodIQ project).
- Struggled occasionally with advanced networking questions (e.g., Transit Gateway routing rules).

### Key Takeaways

#### Technical Knowledge
- Deepened understanding of asynchronous flows and advanced architectural concepts.
- Identified specific networking topics that require further study.

#### Teamwork and Collaboration
- Learned the importance of rapid discussion and finalizing answers within seconds.
- Realized the value of role division: IAM experts handling security, while Serverless experts tackle architecture.
- Gained a realistic simulation of how real-world engineering teams brainstorm and debug system incidents.

### Applying to Work

- Used the advanced architecture questions to verify the accuracy of the asynchronous SQS -> Lambda -> SES flow built during the internship.
- Applied the rapid decision-making skills to debugging backend issues and developing the Dashboard API for the PeriodIQ project.

### Event Experience

Participating in the **AWS Knowledge Battle** was an unforgettable highlight of my internship. It served as a perfect comprehensive review of all the knowledge I had accumulated during my first 5 weeks of self-study.

#### Learning from the Competition
- The head-to-head format was completely different from previous heavy technical seminars, making learning much more engaging.
- Validated that learned theories had fully transformed into reflexes and practical understanding.

#### Networking and Discussions
- The lively atmosphere provided a fantastic networking opportunity to interact with other AWS enthusiasts.
- Cheering and supporting teams created a strong sense of community.

#### Lessons Learned
- Trusting intuition and having quick reflexes are crucial when dealing with complex architectural problems under pressure.
- Identifying knowledge gaps is the first step toward improving as a Cloud Engineer.
- Teamwork and complementary skills are essential for solving difficult technical challenges quickly.
